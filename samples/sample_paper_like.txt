Abstract

We report a small-scale synthesis workflow for evaluating substituted benzoate esters. The procedure records reaction conditions, isolated yield, and simple characterization observations.

Materials and Methods

Benzoic acid, methanol, sulfuric acid, and sodium bicarbonate were used without further purification. Reactions were performed in sealed vials with magnetic stirring. Product mixtures were diluted with ethyl acetate and washed with aqueous sodium bicarbonate.

Results

The methyl benzoate product was isolated as a clear liquid. The reaction at 65 C for 2 hours gave 82% yield. A shorter reaction time gave 54% yield under otherwise identical conditions.

Discussion

Higher conversion was observed when the reaction was heated for the full 2 hours. The evidence indicates that time was a stronger factor than catalyst loading in this simplified experiment.
